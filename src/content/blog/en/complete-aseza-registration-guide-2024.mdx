## What Is the Aqaba Special Economic Zone?

The Aqaba Special Economic Zone (ASEZA) is a multi-sector economic zone established in 2001 to serve as a model for private-sector-led development. It is run by an independent authority with broad administrative and financial powers, allowing it to streamline procedures and serve investors through a single window rather than shuttling between several ministries and departments.

The zone covers virtually the entire city of Aqaba, including the port, the airport, and the industrial, tourism, and commercial areas. This diversity means the range of licensable activities is very wide — from trade and logistics to tourism, services, and light industry.

## Why Choose to Register in Aqaba?

Thousands of investors choose Aqaba for concrete, practical reasons, most notably:

- **Competitive tax incentives:** Low income tax on most activities, with customs exemptions on importing equipment and goods within the zone.
- **Full foreign ownership:** Foreign investors can own their company outright in most sectors without needing a local partner.
- **A single service window:** The zone authority coordinates procedures, dramatically reducing time and complexity.
- **A unique logistics location:** Jordan's only Red Sea port, connecting you to the markets of Asia, Africa, and Europe.
- **Modern infrastructure:** Equipped industrial and logistics zones, with reliable telecoms and power.

Together, these factors lower operating costs and accelerate launch compared with setting up outside the zone.

## A Step-by-Step Overview of Registration

Although the authority has simplified procedures, the path still requires careful preparation. Here is an overview of the steps:

1. **Define the activity and legal structure:** Choose the nature of your business and the most suitable entity (limited liability company, one-person company, foreign company branch, or representative office).
2. **Reserve the trade name:** Confirm the proposed name is available and doesn't conflict with registered names.
3. **Prepare the legal documents:** Draft the articles and memorandum of association and assemble supporting documents.
4. **Submit the application to the authority:** File the complete application with attachments and pay the official fees.
5. **Review and approval:** The authority studies the application and may request clarifications or amendments.
6. **Receive the license:** After approval, you obtain the registration certificate and the license required to start operating.

Each of these steps demands precise knowledge of the requirements, and any small error — such as an imprecise activity description or a missing document — can delay the file by weeks.

## The Expected Timeline

Many investors ask about the actual time to incorporate. Under normal circumstances, with documents complete, things proceed as follows:

- **Days 1–2:** Consultation, choosing the legal structure, preparing documents, and reserving the name.
- **Days 3–4:** Submitting the complete application to the zone authority.
- **Days 5–8:** Reviewing the application and addressing any notes.
- **Days 9–10:** Final approval is issued and the license is collected.

In other words, a well-prepared project can be completed within 5–10 business days. The biggest factor in shortening the timeline is **document readiness and accuracy** from the very start.
